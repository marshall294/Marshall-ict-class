üí° Notes and Detailed Explanations

A computer is an electronic machine that receives data (input), processes it, and gives us information (output). Computers are used all over the world in schools, offices, homes, hospitals, airports, and almost every area of life. Understanding the basic parts of a computer helps us to use it properly.

Main Parts of a Computer:

  • üñ•Ô∏è Monitor: The monitor is the screen of the computer. It shows text, pictures, and videos. It is an output device because it displays what the computer is doing. Example: when you type your name, the letters appear on the monitor.
  • ‚å®Ô∏è Keyboard: The keyboard is used to type letters, numbers, and symbols into the computer. It is an input device because it sends information into the computer. Example: when you press the ‚ÄúA‚Äù key, the computer receives the letter ‚ÄúA‚Äù.
  • üñ±Ô∏è Mouse: The mouse helps us move the arrow (cursor) on the screen, click on items, open files, and drag objects. It is also an input device. Example: dragging a picture from one side of the screen to another.
  • üíæ CPU (Central Processing Unit) / System Unit: This is the brain of the computer. It processes all the information and controls what happens. Without the CPU, the monitor, keyboard, and mouse cannot work properly.

Switching On and Logging In:

To use a computer, press the power button. After starting, some computers ask you to log in by clicking your name or typing a password. This keeps your work safe and private.

Using the Mouse:

  • Click: Press the left button once to select something.
  • Double-click: Press the left button twice quickly to open something.
  • Drag: Hold the left button and move the mouse to move an object.

üåç Real-Life Examples

  • At school, we use computers to learn new topics, draw pictures, and play learning games.
  • At home, people use computers to watch movies, talk to family online, or do homework.
  • In hospitals, doctors use computers to check patient records and machines connected to computers to save lives.
  • At airports, computers help in booking flights and checking passenger tickets.
