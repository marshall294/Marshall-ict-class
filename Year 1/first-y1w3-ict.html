Learning Objective

To use a mouse in different ways.

Success Criteria

  • ✔️ I can click and drag to make objects on a screen.
  • ✔️ I can use a mouse to create a picture.
  • ✔️ I can use a mouse to open a program.

Notes & Explanations

A computer mouse is a small device that helps you control what happens on the screen. It usually has two buttons and sometimes a wheel in the middle.

  • Clicking: Pressing the left button selects or opens something on the screen.
  • Double-clicking: Press the button twice quickly to open a program or file.
  • Dragging: Hold the button down and move the mouse to drag objects around.

Real-life examples:

  • Using the mouse to open “Paint” on the computer.
  • Clicking to choose colours and drawing shapes in Paint.
  • Dragging icons to arrange them neatly on the desktop.

The mouse helps us interact with the computer easily — like a hand guiding what we want to do on the screen.

Key Concepts

  • Mouse: A hand-held device used to move the pointer and select things on a computer screen.
  • Pointer: The arrow on the screen that moves when you move the mouse.
  • Click: Pressing a button on the mouse to make a selection or open a program.
  • Drag: Holding down a mouse button to move an object on the screen.
