Year 1 ICT — Autumn Term Week 4

Topic: Uses of the Keyboard | Teacher: Marshall Jacob

Learning Objective

To identify the keyboard as a part of the computer and explain its uses.

Success Criteria

  • ✔️ I can identify a keyboard on the computer.
  • ✔️ I can tell what the keyboard is used for.
  • ✔️ I can press keys carefully to type letters or numbers.

Notes & Explanations

The keyboard is one of the most important parts of the computer. It is used to type letters, numbers, and symbols into the computer.

  • The keyboard has many small buttons called keys.
  • When we press a key, a letter, number, or sign appears on the screen.
  • We use the keyboard to type words, write our names, or enter commands.

Examples of keyboard use:

  • Typing names and sentences in Word.
  • Typing numbers when doing math work.
  • Using the spacebar to leave spaces between words.
  • Using the Enter key to move to a new line.

Without a keyboard, it would be very hard to tell the computer what to do.

Key Concepts

  • Keyboard: A part of the computer used to type letters and numbers.
  • Keys: The buttons on the keyboard.
  • Typing: Pressing the keys to write on the computer.
  • Spacebar: A long key used to leave spaces between words.
  • Enter Key: Used to move to a new line or give a command.
