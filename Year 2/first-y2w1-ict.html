Year 2 ICT – Week 1: Technology Around Us

Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Recognize the uses of information technology in everyday life.
  • Describe the features of information technology devices.
  • Identify that computers are part of information technology.

Success Criteria

  • ✔️ I can describe some uses of computers.
  • ✔️ I can identify examples of computers.
  • ✔️ I can explain that a computer is part of information technology.

What is Information Technology?

Information Technology (IT) means using computers and digital tools to store, send, and use information. Computers are one of the most common examples of IT.

A computer helps us type, draw, play games, and connect to the internet.
A mobile phone helps us talk, text, and browse the internet.
A printer helps us print out our work from a computer.
A smart TV lets us watch videos and connect to the internet.
A server stores and shares information across networks.

Uses of Computers

  • At School: For learning, typing, showing lessons on a projector.
  • At Home: For homework, games, video calls, and watching movies.
  • At Work: For designing, keeping records, sending emails.
  • In Shops: For scanning items, printing receipts, keeping accounts.
  • In Hospitals: For storing patient information, scanning, and medical machines.

Features of a Computer

  • It has a screen to show information.
  • It has input devices like a keyboard and mouse.
  • It can store information for a long time.
  • It can connect to the internet and share information.

Practical Activity

  1. Look around your classroom. Point to at least 2 examples of computers or IT devices.
  2. With your partner, talk about how you use computers at home.
  3. Draw and label a picture of a computer showing its parts (screen, keyboard, mouse).

Assessment – Check Your Understanding

1. Which of these is an example of information technology?

2. What can we use a computer for?

3. Is a computer part of information technology?
