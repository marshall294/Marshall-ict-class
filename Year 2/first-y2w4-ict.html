Year 2 Computing — Week 4

Topic: Computing Systems and Networks – IT Around Us

Teacher: Marshall Jacob | Duration: 30–40 minutes

Learning Objective

To explain how information technology helps us in everyday life and how different devices can work together.

Success Criteria

  • ✔️ I can demonstrate how IT devices work together.
  • ✔️ I can recognise common types of technology.
  • ✔️ I can say why we use IT.

Teacher Notes — Lesson Flow

  1. Starter (5 mins): Show 3–4 objects (tablet, phone, printer, smart toy). Ask: “Which of these are computers?”
  2. Main (15–20 mins): Explain simply how devices work together (e.g., tablet sends a picture to the printer or uses Wi-Fi to watch a video). Use short role-play or a picture sequence.
  3. Activity (10 mins): Group sorting game — pupils sort pictures into “IT devices” and “Not IT” and draw lines showing which devices could share information.
  4. Plenary (5 mins): Ask pupils to say one way IT helps them at home or school.

Key Concepts (Child-friendly)

  • IT device: Any gadget that uses information technology — like a tablet, computer, phone, or printer.
  • Work together: Devices can share information, for example a tablet sending a picture to a printer.
  • Types of technology: Tablets, laptops, smart toys, cameras, printers, phones.
  • Why we use IT: To learn, play, talk to people, and find information quickly.

Everyday Examples

Example 1: A teacher shows a story on a laptop and pupils watch on the classroom screen.
Example 2: A parent sends a picture to a tablet; the family looks at it together.
Example 3: A smart toy connects to a tablet to play a learning game.
Example 4: A printer prints the pupils’ drawings sent from the classroom computer.

Class Activity — Match & Connect

Materials: Picture cards of devices (phone, book, toy, tablet, printer).

  1. Children work in small groups. Lay out cards and sort into "IT" and "Not IT".
  2. Draw arrows to show how two IT devices could share information (e.g., tablet → printer).
  3. Ask each group to explain one connection in one sentence.

Tip: Keep explanations short and use examples children know (photos, games, stories).

Assessment — Quick Check

Q1: Which of these is an IT device?

Q2: How can a tablet and a printer work together?

Q3: Why do we use IT at school?

Q4: Which is NOT an IT device?

Q5: True or False: A phone can share a photo with a tablet.

Q6: Give one example of technology you use at home. (Oral answer)
Listen for answers like "tablet", "TV", "phone".

Home Learning / Extension

  • Draw a picture of two devices that can share information and label them (e.g., phone → tablet).
  • Tell an adult one reason why we use technology.
