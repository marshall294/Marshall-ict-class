Year 3 ICT – Week 1: Connecting Computers

Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • Explain how digital devices function.
  • Understand the idea of input, process, and output.
  • Give examples of how computers connect and share information.

Success Criteria

  • ✔️ I can explain that digital devices accept input.
  • ✔️ I can explain that digital devices produce output.
  • ✔️ I can follow the input → process → output cycle.

How Do Digital Devices Work?

A digital device is any machine that uses numbers (0s and 1s) to work, like computers, phones, and tablets. Every digital device follows three main steps:

  • Input: Getting data or instructions (example: typing on a keyboard, clicking a mouse, touching a screen).
  • Process: The computer thinks and works on the input (example: calculating, running a program).
  • Output: Showing results (example: displaying text on screen, printing a page, playing a sound).
Typing on a keyboard (input) → computer processes the letters → text appears on the screen (output).
Speaking into a microphone (input) → device processes the sound → music waveform shown on screen (output).
Pressing the camera button (input) → phone processes the photo → picture is shown on screen (output).

Connecting Computers

Digital devices can be connected together to share information. This connection can be through:

  • Wi-Fi: Wireless connection to share internet and files.
  • Cables: Computers can connect with wires.
  • Cloud: Information stored online that devices can access.

Practical Activity

  1. Identify one input and one output device in your classroom.
  2. Draw a diagram showing input → process → output for using a computer.
  3. Work in pairs to explain how two computers can share information (for example, through Wi-Fi).

Assessment – Check Your Understanding

1. Which one is an input device?

2. What is the output when you type on a computer?

3. Which of these shows the correct order?
