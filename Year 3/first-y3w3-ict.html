Year 3 ICT — Autumn Term Week 3

Topic: Connecting Computers | Teacher: Marshall Jacob

Learning Objective

To recognise how digital devices can change the way we work.

Success Criteria

  • ✔️ I can explain how I use digital devices for different activities.
  • ✔️ I can recognise similarities between using digital devices and non-digital tools.
  • ✔️ I can suggest differences between using digital devices and non-digital tools.

Notes & Explanations

Computers and digital devices are all around us. They help us work, learn, and communicate more quickly and efficiently. A digital device is an electronic machine that processes information. Examples include laptops, tablets, and smartphones.

  • How devices change work: Writing a report on a computer is faster and easier to edit than using pen and paper.
  • Similarities: A digital calculator and a normal calculator both help solve math problems.
  • Differences: Sending an email on a computer is faster than posting a handwritten letter.

Real-life examples:

  • A teacher prepares lesson notes with a laptop instead of handwriting them in books.
  • Students research using the internet instead of only reading from printed books.
  • Workers attend online meetings instead of traveling long distances.

Digital devices save time, improve accuracy, and connect us to people around the world.

Key Concepts

  • Digital Device: An electronic tool that processes information (e.g., computer, tablet).
  • Non-Digital Tool: A traditional tool without electronics (e.g., books, pens, abacus).
  • Efficiency: Doing tasks faster and with less effort using technology.
  • Connectivity: The ability of devices to link together and share information.
