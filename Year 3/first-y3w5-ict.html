Year 3 Computing — Week 5

Topic: Computing Systems and Networks – Connecting Computers

Teacher: Marshall Jacob | Duration: 40 minutes

Learning Objective

To explore how digital devices can be connected and to understand how information can move from one device to another within a network.

Success Criteria

  • ✔️ I can demonstrate how information can be passed between devices.
  • ✔️ I can explain the role of a switch, server, and wireless access point in a network.
  • ✔️ I can recognise that a computer network is made up of many connected devices.

Teacher Notes (Step-by-Step Guide)

  1. Step 1 — Introduction (5 mins): Ask pupils, “How do our school computers talk to each other?” Discuss answers.
  2. Step 2 — Explanation (10 mins): Show examples of wired and wireless connections using images or classroom equipment.
  3. Step 3 — Demonstration (10 mins): Let pupils act out being devices — passing “data packets” between each other via a “switch” (student).
  4. Step 4 — Exploration (10 mins): Display the diagram and discuss how each part (switch, server, WAP) helps devices connect.
  5. Step 5 — Assessment (5 mins): Pupils answer interactive questions and review feedback immediately.

Key Notes and Definitions

  • Digital Device: A piece of electronic equipment that can store, process, or send data (e.g., laptop, smartphone, tablet).
  • Network: A group of devices linked together to share information, such as files or internet access.
  • Switch: A device that connects computers within a local network (LAN) and sends data to the right place.
  • Server: A special computer that stores and manages files for others to access.
  • Wireless Access Point (WAP): Lets devices connect to the network wirelessly via Wi-Fi.
  • Router: Connects the local network to the wider internet.

How Devices Connect in a Network

Devices in a network are connected using either cables or Wi-Fi. Each device has a unique address (like a name) so information knows where to go. The switch controls data flow inside the network, while the server stores shared information. The router connects the network to the internet.

Example 1: In the school ICT lab, computers share a printer and internet connection through a switch.
Example 2: At home, phones, tablets, and TVs connect wirelessly to a router using Wi-Fi.

Why Connecting Devices Matters

  • To share resources like printers and files.
  • To access the internet and cloud storage.
  • To allow teamwork through shared folders.
  • To communicate quickly through email and messaging.
Fun Fact: Without networks, every computer would need its own printer and internet line!
