Key Notes and Definitions

  • Network: A group of computers or devices connected together so they can share information.
  • The Internet: A worldwide system of millions of networks all connected together. This is why we say the internet is a network of networks.
  • World Wide Web (WWW): A collection of websites and web pages you can view using the internet. It is not the same as the internet.
  • Router/Modem: A device that helps connect computers to the internet.
  • Packets: Small pieces of data that travel across the internet. Information like videos, emails, and games are broken into packets.
  • IP Address: A unique number that identifies each device on a network.
  • Firewall: A tool that helps block harmful traffic from entering a computer or network.
  • Antivirus Software: A program that protects your computer from harmful software called viruses.
  • Wi-Fi: Wireless signals that allow devices to connect to a network without cables.

How Does the Internet Work?

The internet connects computers using cables, satellites, and Wi-Fi signals. When you send a message, it is broken into small packets. Each packet travels through different routes across networks and is rejoined at the destination. This is how pictures, videos, games, and websites reach you.

Why Do We Need to Protect Networks?

  • Passwords keep our accounts private.
  • Firewalls block hackers and harmful traffic.
  • Antivirus software removes harmful viruses.
  • Without protection, networks can be attacked, and private data can be stolen.

Examples of Networks

  • School Network: All the computers in a school connected to one server.
  • Home Network: Phones, tablets, and laptops sharing the same Wi-Fi.
  • Global Network: Many smaller networks (schools, homes, offices) connected to form the internet.

Common Misconceptions

  • ❌ The internet and the World Wide Web are the same thing. (Truth: the web is only part of the internet.)
  • ❌ Data travels instantly without cables or signals. (Truth: it uses wires, Wi-Fi, and satellites.)
  • ❌ Private networks do not need protection. (Truth: all networks need security.)
