Common Misconceptions

  • ❌ The internet is the same as a computer.

What is a Computer System?

A computer system is a group of parts that work together to take input, process it, and produce output. A system is made of both hardware (physical parts) and software (programs).

Input: Keyboard, mouse, microphone.
Process: The CPU works on the data.
Output: Monitor, speakers, printer.

How Do Systems Communicate?

Computer systems can be connected in a network so they can communicate and share information. Examples include:

  • Wi-Fi networks for sharing internet and files.
  • Wired networks using cables.
  • Cloud systems for storing and accessing information online.

Practical Activity

  1. Label the parts of a computer system on a worksheet (input, process, output).
  2. In groups, discuss how computers in your school connect to share information.
  3. Use a diagram of a network map to explain how devices communicate.
